EE 698M Physics of Semiconductors and Nanostructures - Home Page

This course is directed towards students who wish to understand from fundamentals why semiconductors and nanostructures are employed in various devices that we use in everyday life. We will assume Bachelor level knowledge on electronic devices, mathematical treatment and electrical circuit analysis.

Below is the tentative topics to be covered

Physics of semiconductors and Nanostructures:
  • Classical free-electron models of solids
  • Quantum mechanics of electrons in atoms to nanostructures to bulk solids
  • bandstructure of metals, semiconductors, insulators [e.g. Si, graphene, 2D atomic materials, nanotubes,…]
  • The effective mass theorem, semiconductor heterostructures: Designer quantum wells, wires, dots
  • Electron statistics and dynamics in bands
  • Optical Properties - Dielectric function, plasmonic resonance, joint density of states
  • Electron-photon interaction, optical interband and intraband processes, Excitons, Emission
  • Nanophotonic device example(s): LEDs, Lasers, Photovoltaics
  • Many-particle/collective effects in light-matter interaction and Superconductivity

Books:

  • Solid state physics, N. Ashcroft and D. Mermin, Harcourt, 1976, US
  • Fundamentals of Semiconductors, Yu and Cardona, Springer Verlag (1996)
  • Introduction to Superconductivity, M. Tinkham
